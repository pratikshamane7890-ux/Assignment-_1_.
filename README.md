# Assignment-_1_.Protocol for Analysis of Septin-8 (SEPT8)
1. Title
Detection and Analysis of Septin-8 Protein Expression in Biological Samples
2. Aim
To isolate, detect, and analyze the expression of Septin-8 (SEPT8) protein using molecular and biochemical techniques.
3. Principle
Septin-8 belongs to the septin family of GTP-binding proteins involved in:
Cytoskeleton organization
Cell division
Membrane dynamics
The protocol involves:
Extraction of RNA/protein
Amplification using PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)
Detection through techniques like Western Blot or Immunofluorescence
4. Materials Required
Reagents
TRIzol reagent (for RNA extraction)
Chloroform
Isopropanol
Ethanol (70%)
Reverse transcriptase enzyme
dNTPs
PCR buffer
SEPT8-specific primers
SDS-PAGE reagents
Primary antibody (anti-SEPT8)
Secondary antibody
Equipment
Centrifuge
Micropipettes
Thermal cycler
Gel electrophoresis unit
UV transilluminator
Western blot apparatus
5. Procedure
Step 1: Sample Preparation
Collect tissue or cultured cells
Wash with PBS to remove impurities
Step 2: RNA Extraction
Add TRIzol reagent to the sample
Homogenize thoroughly
Add chloroform and centrifuge
Collect aqueous phase
Precipitate RNA using isopropanol
Wash with ethanol and dissolve in RNase-free water
Step 3: cDNA Synthesis
Take extracted RNA
Add reverse transcriptase and primers
Incubate to synthesize complementary DNA (cDNA)
Step 4: PCR Amplification
Prepare PCR mixture:
cDNA template
SEPT8 primers
dNTPs
Taq polymerase
Run PCR cycles:
Denaturation (~95°C)
Annealing (~55–60°C)
Extension (~72°C)
Step 5: Gel Electrophoresis
Load PCR products on agarose gel
Run electrophoresis
Visualize bands under UV light
Step 6: Protein Extraction
Lyse cells using lysis buffer
Centrifuge to collect protein extract
Step 7: Western Blot Analysis
Run proteins on SDS-PAGE
Transfer to membrane
Block non-specific sites
Add primary antibody (anti-SEPT8)
Add secondary antibody
Detect protein bands
Step 8: Immunofluorescence (Optional)
Fix cells on slides
Add SEPT8 antibody
Add fluorescent secondary antibody
Observe under fluorescence microscope
6. Result
Presence of SEPT8 gene is confirmed by PCR band
Protein expression confirmed by Western blot bands
Localization observed through fluorescence microscopy
7. Precautions
Use RNase-free reagents
Maintain sterile conditions
Handle chemicals carefully
Avoid contamination during PCR
8. Applications
Study of cytoskeletal organization
Cell division research
Neurobiology studies
Disease research (e.g., cancer, neurological disorders)
9. Conclusion
This protocol enables accurate detection and analysis of Septin-8, helping understand its role in cellular structure and function.
